# Fundamental Quality

1. Establish reporting periods, units, source, and freshness.
2. Evaluate revenue and earnings growth without treating one period as a durable trend.
3. Evaluate profitability and capital efficiency using margins, ROE, and available return metrics.
4. Review leverage, liquidity, dilution, and cash conversion when those fields are available.
5. Identify missing data explicitly; do not infer absent cash-flow or governance facts.

## Output

- Growth and profitability
- Capital efficiency
- Balance-sheet resilience
- Earnings-quality observations
- Red flags and missing evidence
- Prove/kill metrics for follow-up research
