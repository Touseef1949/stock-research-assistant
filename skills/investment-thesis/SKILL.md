# Investment Thesis

1. Verify the security, current price, data sources, and as-of times.
2. Establish the market's apparent expectations from valuation and operating trends.
3. Build the bull and bear cases from cited evidence, not generic narratives.
4. State the variant perception: what may be misunderstood or mispriced.
5. Connect catalysts to a plausible timeline.
6. Define thesis falsifiers and monitoring triggers before stating the conclusion.
7. Highlight missing evidence and qualify confidence.

## Output

- One-line thesis
- Variant perception
- Bull case and bear case
- Valuation scenarios
- Catalysts and timeline
- Risks and thesis falsifiers
- Evidence gaps and next diligence actions

Do not issue personalized trade instructions. Present evidence, uncertainty, and decision conditions.
